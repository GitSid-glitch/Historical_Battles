BATTLE OF PRATAPGARH

Home

The Battle of Pratapgarh took place on 10 November 1659, at Pratapgarh Fort in Satara. The battle was fought between the Maratha forces led by Shivaji Raje Bhosale and the Bijapur troops under General Afzal Khan. The Marathas emerged victorious, marking their first significant military triumph against a major regional power. In the aftermath of this victory Shivaji captured 65 elephants, 4000 horses, 1200 camels and 10 Lakh rupees of cash and jewellery.

Afzal Khan, confident of his strength, attempted to lure Shivaji into an open battle. Shivaji, understanding the danger of facing a superior force head-on, instead agreed to meet Afzal Khan for a diplomatic discussion under the guise of a truce. The meeting was arranged at the base of Pratapgarh. Both leaders were to come unarmed, accompanied by a few bodyguards, to negotiate. But Shivaji, aware of Afzal Khan's treachery, prepared carefully. Beneath his flowing robes, he concealed a small weapon called a wagh nakh (tiger claw) and a dagger.

The mighty commander fell, fatally wounded. Outside the tent, Shivaji’s lieutenant, Jiva Mahale, killed Afzal Khan’s bodyguard, Sayyed Banda, who attempted to avenge his master. This quick and decisive action ensured Shivaji’s safety. The death of Afzal Khan was the signal for Shivaji’s hidden forces to launch a surprise attack on the Bijapur army. The victory at Pratapgarh was a turning point in Shivaji’s campaign.The Battle of Pratapgarh marked the beginning of Shivaji’s meteoric rise as a king and military leader. His success in this battle demonstrated the power of strategy, courage, and preparation.

The battle is celebrated as a triumph of intelligence, valor, and the will of a smaller power against a mightier foe. It is a cornerstone in the legacy of Shivaji Maharaj and Maratha pride.
