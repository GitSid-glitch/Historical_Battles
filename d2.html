The Battle of Raichur was fought between the Vijayanagara Empire and the Sultanate of Bijapur in 1520 in the town of Raichur , India. The fort of Raichur was built by Kakatiya king Prataparudra in 1294, and passed on to the Vijayanagara Kingdom after the decline of Kakatiyas.

The Vijayanagara, under Krishnadevaraya, sought to consolidate its control over Raichur.The area was claimed by the Adil Shahi dynasty of Bijapur, a successor state of the Bahmani Sultanate.Krishnadevaraya demonstrated brilliant military strategy and leadership, outmaneuvering the defenders.

The battle was intense and marked by heavy casualties on both sides. The Vijayanagara forces breached the defenses, it resulted in a decisive victory for Vijayanagara forces, and the Bijapur ruler was defeated and pushed across the river Krishna. The defeat weakened the Bijapur Sultanate and heightened tensions between the Deccan Sultanates and Vijayanagara.
